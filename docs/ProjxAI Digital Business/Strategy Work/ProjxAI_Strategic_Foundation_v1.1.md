## Part 1 — The Pivot in One Page

ProjxAI starts as a cash-flow consultancy with three revenue legs running in parallel: a Netlotto retainer that protects the home, a small Phase 1 client pipeline (Safetymate, the pub group, En Perso), and one or two productised AI services that lower the barrier to engagement. The product layer (CEO app, full Da Nang lab, $10M exit positioning) is not the next-90-day game. It is built into the architecture so that *if* the cash-flow business compounds into something exit-shaped, the optionality is preserved — but every decision in the next 12 months is made for revenue, not for exit.

The single biggest unused asset is the Operator Story — a 30+ year Australian digital operator's career, with GM roles, P&L responsibility, and production systems shipped across multiple industries. It must move from buried-in-Michael's-LinkedIn to anchored-front-and-centre on the public site within 30 days. Without that, ProjxAI competes as a generic AI consultancy. With it, ProjxAI is the only Australian AI consultancy founded by an operator who has spent three decades inside the kind of businesses it now serves.

The single biggest infrastructure decision is the AI Opportunity Audit webapp. It is not a docx. It is a live, conversational, on-site assessment that produces an interactive report inside a client portal. It does three jobs at once: it is the positioning proof (we ship, not slideware), the lead-conversion engine, and the engagement-structuring tool. Build it first. Everything else flows from it.

The CEO Intelligence App stays alive but moves to dogfood-first status. Build v0.1 for personal use. Productise only when the consultancy can fund it.

The CEO Coaching service is the surprise high-leverage offering. It can ship next month, requires zero capex, and serves the segment of executives who will not put their data into someone else's app. Productise it.

## Part 2 — Positioning

### The current positioning undershoots

The site currently leads with *"Practical AI systems for Australian SMEs that actually work."* That sentence is honest, accurate, and forgettable. It positions ProjxAI as humble-but-competent, in a market where humble-but-competent loses to specific-and-credentialled. Every prospect lands on the site with the same question: *"Why this consultant, not the other twelve in my LinkedIn inbox?"* The current homepage answers "we're practical." That isn't an answer.

### What we tried in v1.0 and why we're not doing it

The v1.0 strategy proposed leaning on Project ABE — the autonomous bidding engine Michael built at Netlotto — as the credibility anchor. Anonymised. Multi-channel. Outperformed external agencies. Specific numbers. The plan was to ship that line on the homepage hero, the About page, every proposal, every cold email.

After Session 14 reflection, that plan is shelved. Permanently, as far as public copy is concerned. Three reasons:

1. **The IP isn't ours.** Project ABE was built inside Netlotto, with Netlotto data, on Netlotto's spend, against Netlotto's competitors. The architecture, the playbook, the outcomes — all of it sits inside the employment relationship. Public use of it, even anonymised, is at best gray-area, at worst a contract breach.
2. **Anonymisation doesn't anonymise.** "An Australian online lottery brand operating against ASX-listed competitors" describes one company. Anyone in the industry — and anyone within two LinkedIn hops of Michael — would name the client in seconds. That undermines the protection the anonymisation is supposed to provide.
3. **It puts the retainer at risk.** The Netlotto retainer is the cash-flow floor for the next 6–12 months. Foregrounding Project ABE on the public site is the fastest way to surface board-level discomfort about Michael's outside activity. The retainer matters more than the talking point.

Project ABE remains valuable internally — as engineering reference, as the proof to Michael that the methodology works, as the playbook he draws from when scoping client work. It just never leaves the room.

### The new credibility anchor

The Operator Story.

Michael Collicoat is a 30+ year Australian digital operator. GM-level roles. P&L responsibility. Multi-million-dollar marketing budgets owned in-house, not just consulted on. Production systems shipped — marketing automation, data infrastructure, performance reporting, custom integrations. Industries spanning lottery, regulated wagering, hospitality, B2B distribution, and travel. Brisbane-based. Australian businesses, real teams, real revenue, for over three decades.

This is not "I've been around a while." This is the difference between an AI consultant who's read about marketing budgets and an operator who's owned them.

It's a stronger, more durable position than Project ABE was ever going to be. It scales (it doesn't depend on one client). It's verifiable (LinkedIn, board references, employer history). It's defensible (no other Australian SME-focused AI agency is founded by someone with this profile). And it's the same story whether ProjxAI lands at $300k/year as a consultancy or grows into something larger.

This preserves "practical AI" as continuity language while sharpening the differentiation. The "not just consulted on" clause lands the insight without naming any competitor.

### Why this positioning will hold up

- **It's true.** Verifiable career, verifiable industries, verifiable scope. Nothing claimed that can't be backed by LinkedIn, tax records, or board references.
- **It's defensible.** Other AI agencies are founded by AI engineers, designers, or marketers who pivoted. ProjxAI is founded by an operator. That is a genuine and rare position in the Australian SME AI space.
- **It sets buyer expectation correctly.** Prospects who land on the site will expect plain-language conversation, P&L thinking, and execution discipline — not buzzword theatre.
- **It scales with the business.** The operator anchor works at $300k/year as a boutique consultancy and works at $3M/year as a small agency. Project ABE was always going to feel mismatched once the consultancy outgrew its first phase.
- **It's ethically clean.** No NDA edges. No employment-contract gray zones. No anonymisation that doesn't really anonymise.

### What we lose by dropping Project ABE

Be honest: Project ABE was the strongest single proof point. Its absence from public copy means we lose the "outperformed agencies of ASX-listed competitors" line and we lose specific CPA numbers. That is a real cost. The mitigation is the Operator Story itself (breadth and longevity instead of one-project depth) plus the Audit webapp (live capability demonstration instead of historical claim) plus, eventually, ProjxAI's own client case studies (real outcomes that ProjxAI owns outright).

### Decision

**Option C** is recommended for the homepage hero, with Option D's voice carried into the About page. Operator Notes blog series is recommended yes (pending Michael). Project ABE is dropped from public positioning entirely and immediately. Any v1.0 copy that references Project ABE — homepage, About page, founder credibility section, planned case study, planned blog post — is rewritten or removed in Sessions 16–17.

## Part 4 — The Operator Story

*This part replaces v1.0's "The Project ABE Public Story" in full. Project ABE is dropped from public positioning per Session 14. The operator story takes its place.*

### What this section is

The operator story is the canonical narrative ProjxAI uses everywhere — homepage, About page, service pages, proposals, LinkedIn, speaker bios, blog posts, sales conversations. It is not a single paragraph; it is a story with multiple-length variants for different surfaces, all anchored to the same facts and the same voice.

### The career arc

Michael Collicoat has spent 37+ years across digital operations, marketing, technology, and general management in Australian businesses. The progression matters more than any single role:

**Phase 1 — early career (~10 years).** Hands-on technology and digital production. Learning how systems are built, run, broken, and fixed.

**Phase 2 — mid-career (~15 years).** GM and senior leadership roles in digital businesses. P&L ownership. Marketing-budget responsibility. Hiring, scoping vendors, signing off on architectures, sitting in board meetings explaining why the numbers moved.

**Phase 3 — recent (~10+ years).** Operator at the intersection of marketing, tech, and AI. Multi-channel digital operations at scale. Production systems built and run in-house. Direct exposure to the gap between what AI vendors promise and what actually ships.

This arc gives ProjxAI three things no AI agency founded by a younger engineer can match:

1. **P&L instinct.** Knows what a CFO will and won't approve, what a board will and won't underwrite, and how to frame AI investment as a finance decision, not a tech decision.
2. **Operator's bias for shipping.** 30 years of being the person responsible when things either work or don't. No tolerance for slideware. No interest in projects that show well in a deck and never reach production.
3. **Industry breadth.** Lottery, regulated wagering, hospitality, B2B distribution, travel, online services. SME and mid-market scale. Australian regulatory context. The kind of breadth that lets ProjxAI walk into any vertical and orient inside an hour.

### Credibility markers — public

Verifiable, defensible, ethically clean:

- 30+ years in Australian digital operations
- GM-level roles with full P&L responsibility
- Multi-million-dollar marketing budgets owned in-house, not just consulted on
- Production systems shipped — multi-channel marketing automation, performance dashboards, custom integrations, AI tooling

### The pivot moment (founding story)

Two things converged.

First, the major shift in what AI can actually do for businesses — from buzzword to genuinely useful tooling. The capability is now real; the question is no longer "can AI do this?" but "is it worth doing for this business at this stage?"

Second, an operator's recognition that Australian SMEs are about to be sold a lot of AI services by people who have never had to make a P&L work. The next two years will see a wave of AI agencies, AI consultants, and AI tools sold into the Australian SME market by smart engineers and good salespeople who've never sat in front of a CFO defending a marketing spend or a technology investment.

ProjxAI exists to close that gap — to bring AI to Australian SMEs through someone who has spent a career running the kind of business those SMEs are running.

This is not "I quit corporate to start a consultancy." This is "I've watched too many software vendors sell solutions to problems they don't understand. Here is an operator-led alternative."

## Part 6 — Client Portal Architecture

### Why this matters
A client portal does five things at once: (1) protects your IP from being copied off public pages, (2) creates a paywall surface for premium content, (3) becomes the place where every Audit report, proposal, and engagement deliverable lives, (4) gives clients a "logged-in surface" that feels like a SaaS product not a consultant's Dropbox, (5) is the foundation for whatever the CEO app eventually becomes (same auth, same identity model).

### Phase 1 features (ship with Audit webapp)
- Auth: email + magic link (no password). Single user per account in Phase 1.
- "My Audit Report" — the live report from the Audit webapp lives here forever, not as a one-time email link.
- "My Proposals" — proposals generated post-Audit live here. Clickable, accept-with-one-click, e-signature integration.
- "My Engagements" — once a project is live, weekly progress updates posted here. No more "where are we up to?" emails.
- "Resource Library" — gated content. Frameworks, templates, recordings of past workshops, the kind of asset competitors would steal off public pages.
- HubSpot tie-in: every portal action (login, view, click) syncs as an activity on the contact record.

### Phase 2 features (after first 3 paying clients)
- Multi-user per company
- Real-time chat thread with you (Slack-style)
- Workflow status dashboards (live from Make.com or n8n webhooks)
- Billing and invoice download

### Phase 3 features (when Pilot Builds are routine)
- Live workflow performance metrics
- AI usage telemetry
- Documentation library auto-generated per engagement

### Tech approach
- Auth: Clerk or Supabase Auth (Clerk is simpler, costs more; Supabase is broader if you'll use Postgres anyway)
- Database: Supabase Postgres (cheap, scales, plays well with Next.js)
- Frontend: Next.js App Router pages under `/portal/*`
- Asset storage: Supabase Storage or Vercel Blob
- Build cost estimate: 2-3 weeks of focused work for Phase 1
- Run cost: <$50/month at <100 active accounts

### Naming
- Don't call it "client portal." Call it "**ProjxAI Workspace**" or "**The Workshop**" or similar — language that feels like a product, not an admin area.

### "At Your Pace" Exploration — Phase 1 Feature (added Session 24, 1 May 2026)

**Decision:** The post-report experience must not push people straight to a booking. Add an exploration layer to the report inside the Workspace.

**How it works:**
- Each opportunity card has interactive actions: "Tell me more" / "Add more context" / "I'm interested in finding out more about this"
- Clicking opens an AI chat (Claude API) with the full report JSON as context — so every response is specific to their business and their audit answers
- User can ask follow-up questions, request ROI detail, understand implementation steps, all privately and at their own pace
- When ready: "Talk to someone" → Cal.com 15-min booking, with a summary of the conversation automatically attached

**Why this is the right sales motion:**
- People hate cold calls, push emails, and sales pressure
- They research privately, build confidence over days or weeks, then reach out when ready
- By the time they book, they've self-qualified and half-sold themselves — the call is a confirmation, not a pitch
- This replaces the standard nurture email sequence with something genuinely useful

**Where it lives:** `/portal/*` inside projxai.com.au — not a separate product. The email submitted at the report gate becomes the magic-link login. Report persists permanently (not a one-time access token).

**HubSpot tie-in:** Every "Tell me more" click, conversation turn, and "I'm interested" action syncs as an activity on the contact record — so you have full visibility on where each prospect is in their thinking without ever having to chase them.
